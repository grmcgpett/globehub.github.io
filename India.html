India: Land of Diversity

India, officially known as the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia. It is the seventh-largest country by land area, the most populous country, and the world's largest democracy. Renowned for its vibrant culture, colorful festivals, and rich history, India is a tapestry of languages, cuisines, and traditions.

Key Highlights

  • Capital: New Delhi
  • Population: Over 1.4 billion (2025 est.)
  • Official Languages: Hindi and English (India recognizes 22 official languages)
  • Currency: Indian Rupee (₹)
  • Famous Landmarks: Taj Mahal, Qutub Minar, Red Fort, Gateway of India
  • National Symbols: Peacock (bird), Bengal Tiger (animal), Lotus (flower)

Cultural Richness

From the snowy peaks of the Himalayas, to the deserts of Gujarat and to the sun-soaked beaches of Kerala, India’s geography is as diverse as its people. Its festivals such as Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas are celebrated with great enthusiasm. Indian cuisine is famous worldwide, with dishes like biryani, dosa, butter chicken, and samosas tantalizing taste buds everywhere.

Did You Know?

  • India is the birthplace of four major world religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.
  • The game of chess originated in India.
  • The Indian film industry, known as Bollywood, is the world’s largest producer of films.

“Unity in Diversity” truly defines the spirit of India!
